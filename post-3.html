The Link Between Stress and SLE

Stress isn't just a mental state; it triggers physical reactions that can impact the immune system. For those with Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE), stress can lead to an increase in inflammatory markers, potentially causing a flare.

Practical Strategies for Stress Reduction

  • Mindful Movement: Gentle activities like yoga or Tai Chi can lower cortisol levels.
  • Sleep Hygiene: Aim for 8-9 hours of consistent, quality sleep every night.
  • Set Boundaries: Learn to say "no" to commitments that drain your energy.

When to Consult Your Doctor

If you notice persistent or severe symptoms during a stressful period, it is crucial to consult your rheumatologist. They may recommend temporary medication adjustments to help your body cope.
